How did I get interested into web development?

I spent a brief time in customer service providing support for online advertising. Most of the people I helped were small business owners and were trying to figure out online advertising to increase their business. While their business sounded amazing, their website didn't reflect modern online presence.

The odd times where small business owners weren't calling in, I spoke with marketing managers and full stop shops that helped craft and construct large and small businesses. They knew all the acronyms and tips and tricks to make people want to shop at a small ecommerce store. I thought it sounded like a intriguing avenue to take, so I started exploring jobs and skills.

I began to see great community groups pop up like Girl Develop It Ann Arbor, that encourage women to learn and advance in technological careers. I signed up for many classes and workshops ranging from HTML and CSS to WordPress and Intro to Java.

I also studied online at many free and paid services. For paid services, I really enjoyed Skillcrush's Web Developer Blueprint, Team Treehouse lessons, as well as great class deals on Udemy. The list is even great for free service, (maybe worth a blog post in the future :) )
